## Idea 1: "Architectural Noir" — Cinematic Brutalist Luxury

**Design Movement:** Cinematic Noir meets Architectural Brutalism — inspired by high-end real estate photography and film noir aesthetics.

**Core Principles:**
1. Dramatic contrast — near-black backgrounds with sharp white typography and silver metallic accents
2. Photographic dominance — images are the hero, not decoration; they bleed to edges and command attention
3. Restrained elegance — minimal UI chrome, letting the work speak through negative space
4. Cinematic pacing — the scroll experience unfolds like a film, with deliberate reveals and pauses

**Color Philosophy:** A palette rooted in the brand's deep navy (#0D1B2A as the true dark, #1B3A5C as the brand navy, #A0AEC0 silver, #F7F8FA near-white). The darkness isn't just aesthetic — it creates a gallery-like environment where project photos glow against the void, mimicking how luxury homes look at twilight with warm interior lighting against a dark sky.

**Layout Paradigm:** Full-bleed cinematic sections that alternate between immersive photo moments and tight typographic blocks. Asymmetric two-column layouts where text sits in a narrow column while photos expand beyond their container. Horizontal scroll galleries for project detail. No centered hero text — instead, text anchors to the left edge with generous leading.

**Signature Elements:**
1. A thin silver horizontal rule that appears as a recurring divider — echoing the precision of architectural drawings
2. Subtle diamond pattern overlay (from the brochure) used sparingly as a textural watermark on dark sections
3. The SG monogram used as a large watermark element at 5% opacity on key sections

**Interaction Philosophy:** Interactions are deliberate and unhurried. Hover states reveal information through slow opacity transitions. Navigation slides in from the side like a blueprint drawer. Scroll-triggered parallax on hero images creates depth without gimmickry.

**Animation:** Fade-up reveals on scroll with 600ms ease-out timing. Text elements animate in with a slight upward drift (20px). Images scale from 1.02 to 1.0 on entry. Page transitions use a horizontal wipe. The navigation menu slides from the left with staggered item reveals.

**Typography System:** Heading: "Cormorant Garamond" (serif, elegant, architectural) at bold weight for main headings. Body: "Outfit" (geometric sans-serif, clean, modern) at 300-400 weight. The contrast between an elegant serif heading and a clean geometric body creates the tension between tradition and modernity that defines SGR's work.

## Idea 2: "Pacific Northwest Material" — Organic Modernism

**Design Movement:** Pacific Northwest Material Design — inspired by the natural materials (stone, wood, glass) found in West Coast architecture and the landscape of Vancouver Island.

**Core Principles:**
1. Material honesty — textures and surfaces reference real architectural materials
2. Topographic flow — layouts follow organic, terrain-inspired rhythms rather than rigid grids
3. Environmental integration — the website feels like it belongs to the landscape it serves
4. Warm darkness — not cold black, but deep warm tones that evoke evening light through large windows

**Color Philosophy:** Deep charcoal-navy (#0F1923) as the primary dark, warm stone grey (#8B8680) as the material accent, cedar warm (#C4A882) as a tertiary highlight, and crisp white (#FAFAF8) for text. The warmth in the palette references the natural wood and stone materials prevalent in SGR's designs.

**Layout Paradigm:** Sections flow with organic transitions — angled dividers that reference rooflines and terrain. Content blocks are arranged in a masonry-like pattern that echoes stone walls. Wide panoramic photo sections alternate with intimate detail crops. Text blocks are offset from center, creating a sense of architectural asymmetry.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Angled section dividers that reference roof pitches and mountain silhouettes
2. A subtle topographic line pattern used as background texture
3. Warm gradient overlays on photos that evoke golden-hour light

**Interaction Philosophy:** Interactions feel tactile and grounded. Hover effects on project cards create a subtle shadow lift as if picking up a material sample. Scroll reveals feel like walking through a home — rooms (sections) reveal themselves naturally.

**Animation:** Staggered fade-in with parallax depth layers. Photos slide in from alternating sides. Section transitions use a gentle parallax offset. Numbers count up when statistics enter view. Navigation items have a subtle underline that draws from left to right.

**Typography System:** Heading: "DM Serif Display" (warm serif with personality). Body: "Source Sans 3" (humanist sans-serif, excellent readability). The serif heading carries warmth and craft, while the body font ensures professional clarity.

## Idea 3: "Blueprint Precision" — Technical Luxury

**Design Movement:** Swiss International Style meets Architectural Blueprint — the precision of technical drawings elevated to luxury presentation.

**Core Principles:**
1. Grid precision — every element aligns to an invisible architectural grid
2. Technical beauty — fine lines, precise spacing, and measured proportions
3. Portfolio-first — the work is presented with museum-quality framing
4. Monochromatic authority — a restrained palette that lets craftsmanship speak

**Color Philosophy:** Pure deep navy-black (#0A1628) as the void, architectural silver (#B0B8C4) for structural elements, blueprint blue (#1B3A5C) for brand moments, and pure white (#FFFFFF) for text. The palette is deliberately restrained — like an architect's presentation board where the drawings and photos are the color.

**Layout Paradigm:** A strict modular grid inspired by architectural drawing sheets. Content is organized in precise columns with mathematical spacing. Full-width photo sections break the grid dramatically — like a rendering pinned over blueprints. Sidebar navigation with section indicators that track scroll position.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Fine 1px silver lines used as grid references throughout — visible but subtle, like construction lines
2. Section numbers in large, light typography (01, 02, 03) anchoring each page section
3. A thin progress bar at the top of the page that fills as you scroll

**Interaction Philosophy:** Precise and mechanical. Hover states snap rather than ease. Cursor changes to a crosshair on interactive elements. Project cards flip to reveal details like turning a blueprint page.

**Animation:** Sharp, precise movements with 300ms cubic-bezier timing. Elements slide in along grid lines (purely horizontal or vertical, never diagonal). Text appears character by character for key headings. Section transitions use a clean vertical reveal.

**Typography System:** Heading: "Space Grotesk" (geometric, technical, modern). Body: "IBM Plex Sans" (engineered precision, excellent at small sizes). Both fonts carry technical DNA — they feel like they belong on architectural specifications while remaining highly legible.
